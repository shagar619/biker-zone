Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet has been through a tumble, it’s a one-and-done situation. Replace it, even if it still looks like it’s ready to take on the world. Helmets are like superheroes—once they’ve saved you, they retire gracefully.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

When you retrieve your bike from storage, give it a once-over. Inspect all bolts and screws to ensure they’re tightened securely. Loose fasteners can compromise the bike’s structural integrity and safety.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Obey traffic signals and stop signs. Red means stop, green means go—just like in a car.Ride in a straight line. No zigzagging like a caffeinated squirrel between parked cars.
